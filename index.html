What the official documents say

The billing facts

01

Work and Codex share usage

OpenAI's current Codex help article says usage from Codex, ChatGPT Work, ChatGPT for Excel and Workspace Agents draws from the same agentic usage and credit pool when available.

Official Codex help article ↗
02

The pricing and limits are also shared

OpenAI's pricing documentation states that Work usage inside ChatGPT uses the same pricing, credits and usage limits as Codex.

Official pricing documentation ↗
03

Credits cross feature boundaries

After included usage is exhausted, supported features can draw from a shared credit balance. Credits added through Codex may be used by Work or other supported features.

Official credits FAQ ↗
04

Auto top-up can be triggered by the shared balance

Eligible Plus and Pro users may enable Auto top-up. When the shared balance drops below the chosen minimum, OpenAI can charge the saved payment method to restore the target balance.

Official Auto top-up terms ↗
05

Credits expire and are generally non-refundable

OpenAI says purchased credits are generally non-refundable and expire 12 months after purchase. Exceptions may apply where required by law.

Official credits FAQ ↗
06

Image generation can burn limits faster

OpenAI says image generation uses the same general agentic limits and consumes included usage roughly three to five times faster on average, depending on quality and size.

Why consumers may miss it

Detailed help and pricing pages

One allowance underneath

  • Shared agentic usage allowance

A material billing relationship is disclosed, but several clicks away from the purchase summary and mode switch.

Consumer impact, stated carefully

For a subscriber who previously used their included agentic allowance mainly for Codex, adding Work to the same pool means Work activity can reduce the remaining Codex capacity. The subscription price may stay unchanged while more products compete for the same metered resource.

This does not prove that every customer receives less value. Customers who never use Work may see no practical reduction, and GPT-5.6 Sol currently has the same published Codex credit rate as GPT-5.5.

Accuracy before ammunition

What is proven, inferred and unproven

Documented fact

Work and Codex share usage, pricing, limits and credits.

Directly stated in current OpenAI documentation.

Reasonable inference

Using Work can leave less included capacity for Codex.

This follows from both products drawing from one allowance.

Not established

Ordinary Chat MCP calls automatically count as Work.

No official documentation found currently establishes that edge case.

Not established

OpenAI deliberately intended to deceive customers.

The concern here is disclosure quality and billing design, not an unsupported claim about intent.

What fair disclosure should look like

Five requested fixes

  1. Put the shared-meter warning beside the Chat/Work control.Users should know before starting a task that Work can reduce Codex capacity.
  2. Show an estimated usage range before execution.Especially for long-running, visual, scheduled or multi-tool tasks.
  3. Name the feature that consumed each credit.Usage history and top-up receipts should distinguish Work, Codex, Excel and other agentic tools.
  4. Provide separate pools or user-configurable budgets.Customers should be able to reserve purchased credits for a chosen product.
  5. Offer a fair review and refund process.Especially where customers could not reasonably understand that a newly introduced feature shared an existing allowance.
