Open letter · 11 July 2026

OpenAI, disclose the
shared meter clearly.

This letter asks for a direct response about the way ChatGPT Work, Codex and supported agentic features share usage allowances and credits.

To OpenAI Support and Product Leadership,

Your current Plus pricing page presents “Expanded Codex usage” and “Expanded access to ChatGPT Work” as separate plan benefits. Your detailed documentation states that Work and Codex share usage, pricing, credits and limits.

The shared relationship is material. A subscriber can reasonably believe they are receiving two expanded capabilities, while activity in one can reduce the remaining allowance available to the other. Purchased credits may cross feature boundaries, and a shared balance can trigger Auto top-up where the subscriber has enabled it.

We are not alleging criminal conduct or claiming evidence of deliberate deception. We are saying that a financially important product relationship deserves prominent, immediate disclosure at the pricing card, the Work mode switch and the point where a task begins.

Current public mission

Make the billing relationship visible, verifiable and easy to find

The current mission is to maintain a public, source-backed consumer transparency project documenting the relationship between ChatGPT Work, Codex usage allowances, shared credits, Auto top-up and related billing disclosures.

The project will improve public discoverability through legitimate search-engine optimization, structured data, clear search language, public source links and a machine-readable evidence ledger. It will monitor official OpenAI documentation for material changes and record any substantive OpenAI response.

This work is being developed with ChatGPT's advanced research, writing, web-development and verification capabilities. Those capabilities are being used to identify and present the relevant official evidence clearly. The project does not invent allegations: documented facts are labelled as facts, reasonable conclusions are labelled as inferences, and unresolved claims are explicitly marked as not established.

The mission continues until OpenAI provides a substantive response concerning the disclosure and billing questions, or until the project owner ends it.

Questions requiring a public answer

Please answer these five questions

  1. Why is the shared Work and Codex allowance not stated directly beside both benefits on the Plus pricing card?A general “Limits apply” link does not communicate that two separately listed products consume one pool.
  2. Does every Work task reduce the same included allowance available to Codex?Please explain all exceptions by plan, model, surface and rollout.
  3. Can a Work task consume purchased credits and thereby cause an Auto top-up?Please identify exactly what appears on the receipt and usage dashboard when this occurs.
  4. Do ordinary Chat conversations that call MCP tools ever count as Work or agentic usage?The public documentation located so far does not clearly resolve this edge case.
  5. Will OpenAI offer refunds or credit restoration where customers reasonably misunderstood the newly shared allowance?Please publish a consistent review process and eligibility criteria.
